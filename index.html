Why Visit

The kind of place you come back to.

Most people find Willow Waterhole almost by accident, then wonder how they never knew it was here. Quiet water, open sky, and birdsong instead of traffic, cared for so it stays this way for the next generation. Come once and you'll be back.

Six Lakes. Endless Discovery.

See the full park map →

Each lake has its own character. Find the one that becomes yours.

01

Westbury Lake

The easiest place to start: a walking loop, the fishing pier, and the beloved Turtle Bridge.

 02

Triangle Lake

The front porch of the park: landmark sign, prairie garden, and a grand old live oak.

 03

Scout Lake

Where water, nature, and engineering come together along the bayou.

 04

Heron Lake

The best place to slow down and watch wildlife under the ancient oak.

 05

Willow Lake

Big water views and wetlands at the heart of the Greenway.

 06

Prairie Lake

Home to the Prairie Trail and Houston's spring wildflower displays.
New & Worth the Trip

Walk the new Prairie Boardwalk.

A half-mile elevated boardwalk now carries you out into a 20-acre native coastal prairie, one of the last of its kind in Texas and home to plants found almost nowhere else on Earth. A grand opening celebration is coming soon.

Learn & Grow

A living classroom in southwest Houston.

Willow Waterhole is a place to learn by doing. Join the monthly bird survey that's run since 2007, lend a hand at a volunteer workday, walk the prairie among rare and endangered plants, or follow along with Wildlife Wednesday. There's always something new to discover.
