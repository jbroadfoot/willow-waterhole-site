Why Visit

The kind of place you always come back to.

Many people find Willow Waterhole almost by accident, then wonder how they never knew it was here. Whether you're here to catch the morning herons, fish with the family, or find a quiet sunset trail, it's a different kind of Houston park.

Six Lakes. One Greenway.

See the full park map →

A connected chain of lakes, each with its own character, wildlife, and views.

01

Westbury Lake

Stroll the popular walking loop, cast a line off the fishing pier, or spot local wildlife at the beloved Turtle Bridge. This is the perfect place to start your journey.

 02

Triangle Lake

Serving as the park's welcoming "front porch," this vibrant space features our landmark entry sign, a native prairie garden, and a grand old live oak.

 03

Scout Lake

Discover quiet, shaded shorelines where the lakes meet the bayou — and see the Greenway's flood-protection work up close.

 04

Heron Lake

Slow down, settle in, and watch the waterbirds from a shaded bench tucked beneath a massive, ancient oak tree.

 05

Willow Lake

Experience expansive waters and vibrant wetlands right at the heart of the Greenway. This deep-water haven is a premier destination for migratory waterbirds.

 06

Prairie Lake

Wander through an open gateway to the coastal prairie, offering front-row seats to Houston's spectacular spring wildflower displays.
New & Worth the Trip

Walk the new Prairie Boardwalk.

A half-mile elevated boardwalk now carries you out into a 20-acre native coastal prairie, one of the last of its kind in Texas and home to plants found almost nowhere else on Earth. A grand opening celebration is coming soon.

From a vision to one of Houston's most remarkable parks.

Twenty-five years ago, this was flat ground. What began as a regional flood-control project became something more — 300 acres of lakes, prairie, and trails where wildlife thrives and the community gathers, free to everyone, every day.
