Why Visit

The kind of place you always come back to.

Many people find Willow Waterhole almost by accident, then wonder how they never knew it was here. Whether you're here to catch the morning herons, fish with the family, or find a quiet sunset trail, it's a different kind of Houston park.

Six Lakes. One Greenway.

See the full park map →

A connected chain of lakes, each with its own character, wildlife, and views.

01

Westbury Lake

Stroll the popular walking loop, cast a line off the fishing pier, or spot local wildlife at the beloved Turtle Bridge. This is the perfect place to start your journey.View Trail Details →

 02

Triangle Lake

Serving as the park's welcoming "front porch," this vibrant space features our landmark entry sign, a native prairie garden, and a grand old live oak.View Trail Details →

 03

Scout Lake

Discover quiet, shaded shorelines where the lakes meet the bayou, and see the Greenway's flood-protection work up close.View Trail Details →

 04

Heron Lake

Slow down, settle in, and watch the waterbirds from a shaded bench tucked beneath a massive, ancient oak tree.View Trail Details →

 05

Willow Lake

Experience expansive waters and vibrant wetlands right at the heart of the Greenway. This deep-water haven is a premier destination for migratory waterbirds.View Trail Details →

 06

Prairie Lake

Wander through an open gateway to the coastal prairie, offering front-row seats to Houston's spectacular spring wildflower displays.View Trail Details →

25th Anniversary

A quarter century of turning a vision into one of Houston's most remarkable parks.

Twenty-five years ago, this was flat ground. What the community built here (291 acres of lakes, prairie, trails, and wildlife habitat, free to everyone, every day) is something worth stopping to celebrate.
