2001–2026 · 25th Anniversary

A quarter century of turning a vision into one of Houston's most remarkable parks.

Twenty-five years ago, this was flat ground and a flood-control plan. Look what the community built.

25years of the Conservancy
6lakes excavated
8 milesof trails completed
291acres protected

Some parks are simply found. This one was built, lake by lake, trail by trail, tree by tree, by a community that believed a flood-control basin could become something extraordinary. On our 25th anniversary, we're looking back at how far we've come, and forward to what's next.

Then & Now

The same ground, transformed.

Two views of the exact same land. On the left: bare fields, industry, and a gravel operation. On the right: six lakes, restored prairie, eight miles of trails, and habitat for hundreds of species, all free to everyone, every day.

What hasn't changed is the community that made it, and keeps making it, possible.

The Story

From plan to place.

2001: The vision

The Brays Bayou Association founds the Conservancy with a bold idea: build a major public park on top of new flood-control basins.

2005: First runs

Fun Runs began at the greenway.

2007: Stewardship takes root

Eagle Scout Program, conservation workdays, and Audubon monthly bird counts started.

2009: A plan on paper

Greenway Master Plan published.

2011: Planting for the future

Trees for Houston began planting tribute groves.

2012: A stage is chosen

Greenway selected as the site for the next Levitt Music Pavilion.

2013: The music begins

MusicFEST began at Willow Waterhole.

2015: A course to run

High school cross-country meets began.

2019: Flood control complete

HCFCD completed construction of all flood-control structures; six lakes fully functional for stormwater detention.

2020: The prairie protected

HCFCD completed conservation work on the 15-acre Native Prairie Preserve.

2025: Milestones

The number of Eagle Scout and Gold Award projects reached 80. The Great Blue Heron sculpture was installed on the Audubon Trail at Heron Lake.

2026: Celebration

The Native Prairie Preserve Boardwalk opened. WWGC celebrated its 25th Anniversary.

The next 25: Just getting started

Expanded education programming, deeper community partnerships, and continued restoration of this rare urban ecosystem.
