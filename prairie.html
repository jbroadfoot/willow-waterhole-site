The Native Coastal Prairie

One of the last of its kind, right here in Houston.

Only one percent of the Texas coastal prairie remains. Willow Waterhole protects twenty acres of it, and a new boardwalk lets you walk right into it.

Before Houston was a city, tallgrass coastal prairie stretched across more than nine million acres of the upper Texas coast. Today almost all of it is gone, plowed, paved, and built over. What survives at Willow Waterhole is a rare remnant of that lost landscape, hidden in plain sight beside a stormwater basin in southwest Houston, and now open for anyone to explore.

Rare & Protected

Come see a flower that grows almost nowhere else on Earth.

The preserve shelters three species hanging on at the edge of survival, including a federally endangered wildflower you can see in only a handful of places in the world. Mima mounds, the low rises that ripple across the prairie, create the bare, sandy soils these rare plants depend on.

Endangered

Texas Prairie Dawn

A tiny annual wildflower with small yellow blooms, listed as endangered since 1986 and discovered here during the original survey. It grows just inches tall in the sparse soils between mima mounds.

Threatened

Houston Camphor Daisy

A threatened plant with bright yellow flowers and a camphor scent. Fewer than twenty populations remain in its native range across Harris and Galveston Counties.

Threatened

Texas Windmill Grass

A threatened grass that grows at the base of the mima mounds, part of the delicate web of native plants the preserve was created to protect.

A Hidden Discovery

Found by accident, saved on purpose.

When the Harris County Flood Control District purchased land east of South Post Oak Road near Gasmer Drive for stormwater detention, they discovered something unexpected: fifteen acres of untouched native coastal prairie. Because only one percent of this ecosystem remains in Texas, the find was extraordinary.

The prairie was fenced off, preserved, and surveyed by local environmental experts and the U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service. They identified 265 native plant species, an astonishing diversity for so small an area, including one endangered and two threatened species found almost nowhere else on Earth.

New: The Prairie Boardwalk

A half-mile walk into a rare world.

Thanks to a 2023 grant from the Texas Parks & Wildlife Department, a half-mile elevated boardwalk and educational signage now carry visitors out into the heart of the preserve, without disturbing the fragile ground beneath.

Along the way, interpretive panels tell the story of the prairie, its plants and pollinators, the birds of the Central Flyway, and the engineering that made it all possible. An outdoor classroom at the center welcomes school groups and visitors of every age.

Why It Matters

A small prairie does big work.

Wildlife habitat

Coyotes, raccoons, birds, snakes, and countless insects make their home in the prairie, a diverse community of life in the middle of the city.

Native pollinators

As pollinator populations decline worldwide, the prairie supports bees, butterflies, bats, birds, and beetles essential to our food and natural world.

Flood control

The deep roots of prairie plants help rainwater soak in during big storms, holding water and releasing it slowly to protect nearby neighborhoods.

Cleaner water & stored carbon

Prairie plants absorb excess nutrients to improve water quality, and their deep roots pull carbon from the air and store it in the soil.

Keeping It Wild

Tended by experts, and a few goats.

The Harris County Flood Control District maintains the preserve through careful mowing, woody-plant suppression, and invasive-species removal, all guided by the Willow Waterhole Prairie Management Plan.

In a memorable twist, a 2023 pilot brought in a herd of about 300 goats to graze the prairie naturally, an eco-friendly way to keep invasive growth in check and the native plants thriving.

Come walk the prairie.

The boardwalk is open every day, dawn to dusk. Park at the Prairie Lake lot on Gasmer Drive and follow the signs to the Native Prairie Preserve.
