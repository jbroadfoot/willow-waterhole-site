Plan Your Visit

Come as you are. Stay as long as you like.

A 291-acre park and stormwater detention complex in southwest Houston. Six lakes, eight miles of ADA trails, native coastal prairie, and one of the city's best birding sites.

Dawn–Duskopen every day
Freeadmission, always
No restroomson site — plan ahead
Leashed dogswelcome

There's no single attraction and no required route — just follow the water and let your visit unfold.

Getting Here & Parking

Park where your day begins.

Don't just pick the nearest lot — pick the one that matches what you came to do.

Clematis Lots — quiet & wild

11200 & 11300 blocks of Clematis Ln

Best for birding, wildlife viewing, quiet walking, and Turtle Bridge access. The north lot is closest to Willow Lake (Kinder Foundation Trail); the south lot serves Willow Lake (Kinder Foundation Trail).

Get directions →

Dryad Drive Lot — basecamp

5300 Dryad Dr — main entrance

Best for picnics, hiking loops, kayaking, hammocking, and longer visits. Closest to Westbury Lake and the Bob Schwartz Gazebo, with access to the Kevin Shanley, Bluebonnet, Karen Cullar, and Pier Trails.

Get directions →

Gasmer Drive Lot — the prairie

4700 block of Gasmer Dr

Closest to Prairie Lake and the Cullen Foundation Trail — your gateway to the Native Prairie Preserve and the new boardwalk, across South Post Oak Road from the other five lakes.

Get directions →

The simple rule

Can't decide?

Quiet wildlife experience → Clematis. Longer stay or basecamp → Dryad. The prairie and boardwalk → Gasmer. Full experience → start anywhere and let the trails connect it all.

What to Bring

Pack light. Pack smart.

  • Water — more than you think you'll need
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Sunscreen and a hat
  • Bug spray
  • Binoculars and a camera
  • Optional: picnic supplies, a blanket, a book, or your own bike, kayak, or fishing gear

Based on availability, the Conservancy lends fishing poles, binoculars, and other gear at no cost — donations welcome. Email before your visit to reserve.

Know Before You Go

A few things worth knowing.

No restrooms on site

Plan accordingly before you arrive. The nearby Dr. Walker Library offers indoor space and a place to cool off.

Pack it in, pack it out

There are no trash services on the trails. Please carry out everything you bring and help keep the park beautiful.

Know your poison ivy

"Leaves of three, let it be." It's a normal part of the ecosystem — give it space and stay on established paths.

Dogs on leash

Dogs are welcome and loved here. Please keep them leashed at all times and use the waste stations.

Share the trail

Trails are shared by walkers, runners, and cyclists. Ride at a friendly pace and give a heads-up when passing.

Turtle Bridge etiquette

Watch the turtles from the bridge — and please, no bread. Corn or peas only, if you feed them at all.

Slow Down

Try forest breathing.

Some visits aren't about miles. Find a bench, a shady patch of grass, or a hammock spot, and let the park do the rest. We keep a simple forest-breathing exercise on our Things to Do page.

How Long Should I Stay?

Three ways to spend your time.

60–90 minutes

Loop one lake, pause for a birding stop, and finish at the Bob Schwartz Gazebo. The perfect first taste.

2–4 hours

The full basecamp: picnic by Westbury Lake, paddle when conditions allow, hang a hammock, and walk the prairie boardwalk.

4+ hours

The full-day naturalist: all six lakes, the prairie, forest breathing, and a golden-hour finish by the water.

In the Area

Make a morning or a day of it.

Coffee & morning start: Tapester's Grill for a quick breakfast and coffee, or Fioza Café for a slower start.

Quick meals: Annie's Hamburgers, The Hungry Farmer BBQ, and Lo Nuevo De Jalisco — a great taqueria very close to the park.

Evening: A Drop of the Creator — distillery, cocktails, pizza, and outdoor seating.

Supplies: Kroger and Foodarama for groceries and picnic basics; Academy Sports + Outdoors for fishing gear, chairs, coolers, and sunscreen.

A cool-down stop: the Dr. Walker Library, just minutes away, with indoor space and restrooms.

First-Timer's Top 5

If you only do five things.

Turtle Bridge at sunrise

The park's most loved spot. Come early, lean on the rail, and watch the turtles gather in the morning light.

The Great Blue Heron sculpture

Heron Lake's newly installed landmark, standing watch over the park's best birding water.

The Prairie Boardwalk

A 1/3-mile walk into one of the last coastal prairies in Texas — now open, and unlike anywhere else in Houston.

The wide-open Prairie Lake view

Big sky, open water, and spring wildflowers — the closest thing to the Texas horizon inside the Loop's orbit.

The Schwartz Gazebo at golden hour

End your visit where the community gathers — the gazebo glows as the sun drops behind Westbury Lake.
