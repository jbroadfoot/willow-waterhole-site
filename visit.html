Discover Willow Waterhole

A unique urban greenway in southwest Houston.

Once a flood-control reservoir, Willow Waterhole has grown into one of Houston's most distinctive public greenspaces: six connected lakes, miles of shaded trails, and a native prairie, all within the Westbury neighborhood.

Park at a Glance
  • 291 Acres
  • Six Connected Lakes
  • 8.5 Miles of ADA-Friendly Trails
  • 10 Scenic Islands
  • Westbury Lake, the largest lake in the park
  • 15 Acres of Native Prairie

Seasonal Visitor Guide

What to expect right now.

Summer
July – August

Hot days, cool mornings, and peaceful lakes.

Summer is a wonderful time to visit Willow Waterhole. Early mornings offer cooler temperatures, beautiful light, and active wildlife, while the park remains enjoyable throughout the day with a little planning.

  • Early mornings offer cooler temperatures, beautiful light, and active wildlife.
  • Willow and Heron Lakes are great places to watch wading birds throughout the summer.
  • The Prairie Boardwalk is especially beautiful in the soft morning and evening light.
  • Evening walks become especially enjoyable as temperatures begin to cool.
  • Bring plenty of water, especially during the hottest part of the day.

Before You Arrive

Know before you go.

A few simple tips will help you make the most of your visit.

What to Bring

Must-Haves
  • Water, especially during summer
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Sun protection
  • Bug spray
Nice to Have
  • Binoculars for birdwatching
  • Camera
  • Picnic lunch or snacks
  • Fishing gear (or borrow ours, email ahead)

Restrooms

There are no public restrooms within Willow Waterhole. Plan ahead before arriving. The nearby Dr. Shannon Walker Neighborhood Library offers indoor restrooms and is just minutes from the main entrance during normal operating hours.

Accessibility

More than 8.5 miles of paved, ADA-accessible trails connect all six lakes. The Westbury Lake Fishing Pier and Prairie Boardwalk are also accessible.

Good to Know

  • Park hours: Dawn to Dusk
  • Dogs welcome on leash
  • Free parking throughout the park
  • Please help keep the park clean
While You're Here

A few things worth knowing.

Help keep Willow Waterhole beautiful for everyone.

Wildlife First

Watch wildlife from a respectful distance and avoid disturbing nests, habitats, and other natural areas.

Stay on the Trails

Help protect native prairie and wetland habitats by staying on established trails and boardwalks.

Leave No Trace

Please pack out everything you bring with you and help keep the park clean for the next visitor.

Dogs on Leash

Dogs are welcome throughout the park. Please keep them leashed and use the waste stations provided.

Share the Trail

Walkers, runners, cyclists, photographers, and wildlife all share the trails. Be courteous and announce when passing.

Turtle Bridge Tip

If feeding turtles at Turtle Bridge, offer corn or peas only, never bread.
