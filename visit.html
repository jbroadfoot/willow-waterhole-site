Getting Oriented

A few things to know first.

First Visit?

Start at Dryad Drive

  • Park at 5300 Dryad Dr
  • Walk to Westbury Lake
  • Cross Turtle Bridge
  • Follow the water

One loop takes about 60 to 90 minutes and shows you the best of the park.

How Long to Stay?

Pick your pace

  • 60–90 min: one lake, Turtle Bridge, gazebo
  • Half day: two or three lakes, picnic, boardwalk
  • Full day: all six lakes, prairie, birding, golden hour

There's no wrong answer. Every length of visit is worth it.

Best Time to Visit?

Early mornings win

  • Wildlife most active at dawn
  • Trails quietest on weekday mornings
  • Summer: arrive before 9am for the best experience
  • Photography: golden hour at sunrise and sunset

The park is open every day, dawn to dusk.

Seasonal Visitor Guide

What to expect right now.

Summer 2026
July – August

Hot days, early mornings, and the best birding of the year.

Summer at Willow Waterhole rewards early risers. The first two hours after sunrise are when wildlife is most active and the light is at its best.

  • Arrive before 9am for cooler trails and active wildlife
  • Shaded loops around Heron and Willow Lakes stay comfortable all day
  • Watch Willow Lake wetlands for roseate spoonbills and herons
  • The Prairie Boardwalk is beautiful in the early morning light
  • Bring more water than you think you need

Before You Arrive

Know before you go.

What to Bring

Must-haves

  • Water, more than you think you need
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Sun protection and a hat
  • Bug spray

Good to have

  • Binoculars
  • Camera
  • Picnic supplies
  • Fishing gear, or borrow ours (email ahead)

Restrooms and Access

No restrooms on site. Plan ahead. The Dr. Walker Library nearby has indoor space and restrooms.

Eight miles of ADA-accessible trails connect the lakes. The fishing pier at Westbury Lake is ADA-compliant. The Prairie Boardwalk is concrete and accessible.

While You're Here

A few things worth knowing.

Pack it in, pack it out

No trash service on the trails. Carry out everything you bring.

Dogs on leash

Dogs are welcome. Keep them leashed and use the waste stations.

Wildlife etiquette

Watch from a distance. No feeding wildlife except turtles at Turtle Bridge: corn or peas only, never bread.

Trail courtesy

Trails are shared. Cyclists, call out when passing. Everyone, give wildlife space.

Poison ivy

Leaves of three, let it be. Stay on established paths and you'll be fine.

No restrooms on site

Plan ahead. Dr. Walker Library is nearby and has facilities.

In the Area

Make a morning or a day of it.

For coffee and breakfast before the park: Tapester's Grill or Fioza Café. Quick meals nearby include Annie's Hamburgers, The Hungry Farmer BBQ, and Lo Nuevo De Jalisco. For an evening out after your visit, A Drop of the Creator offers cocktails, pizza, and outdoor seating.

For supplies: Kroger and Foodarama for picnic basics, Academy Sports for fishing gear and sunscreen. The Dr. Walker Library is just minutes away and offers a cool, quiet stop with restrooms.
