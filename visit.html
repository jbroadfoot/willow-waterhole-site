Getting Oriented

A few things to know first.

First time here?

Start at the Dryad Drive Lot (5300 Dryad Dr). Walk to Westbury Lake, cross Turtle Bridge, and follow the water. One loop is about 60 to 90 minutes and gives you the best first impression of the park.

How long should I stay?

60 to 90 min: one lake loop, Turtle Bridge, the gazebo. Half day: two or three lakes, picnic, the boardwalk. Full day: all six lakes, prairie, birding, golden-hour finish.

Best time to visit?

Early morning: wildlife is most active, trails are quiet. Weekday afternoons: least crowded. Summer: come early or late to avoid peak heat. Shaded trails around Westbury, Heron, and Willow Lakes stay comfortable all day.

Seasonal Guide

What to expect right now.

Summer 2026

Hot days, early mornings, and the best birding of the year.

Summer at Willow Waterhole rewards early risers. Wildlife is most active in the first two hours after sunrise, the light is beautiful for photography, and shaded trails along Westbury, Heron, and Willow Lakes stay comfortable well into the morning.

  • Arrive early. The park is at its best between 6:30 and 9am in July and August.
  • Bring more water than you think you need.
  • Shaded trail loops around Heron Lake and the Kinder Foundation Trail offer comfortable walking throughout the day.
  • Keep an eye on Willow Lake wetlands for roseate spoonbills, herons, and shorebirds.
  • The boardwalk at Prairie Lake is open and beautiful in the early morning light.
Plan Your Time

How long do you have?

Quick Visit (60–90 minutes)

Perfect first taste. Park at Dryad, walk the Westbury Lake loop, stop at Turtle Bridge, rest at the Schwartz Gazebo. One loop, one lake, and you'll understand why people keep coming back.Best lot: Dryad Drive

Half Day (2–4 hours)

Two or three lakes, a picnic by the water, and a walk on the Prairie Boardwalk. Enough time to feel like you've really explored, not just passed through.Best lots: Dryad Drive + Gasmer Drive

Full Day (4+ hours)

All six lakes, the full prairie boardwalk, birding at the wetlands, and a golden-hour finish. Bring lunch, a camera, and no agenda.Best lots: start anywhere, follow the trails

Dryad Drive Lot

Main entrance 5300 Dryad Dr

The park's basecamp. Closest to Westbury Lake, the Bob Schwartz Gazebo, Turtle Bridge, and the fishing pier. Access to the Kevin Shanley, Bluebonnet, Karen Cullar, and Pier Trails. Best lot for first-timers, picnics, kayaking, and longer visits. Also your starting point for reaching Scout and Triangle Lakes.

See Westbury Lake details → Get directions →

Clematis North Lot

Birding and wildlife 11200 block of Clematis Ln, south of South Willow Drive

Closest to Willow Lake and Heron Lake. Access to the Kinder Foundation Trail and Audubon Trail. The top choice for birding and wildlife viewing: Willow Lake's wetlands and Heron Lake's shaded shorelines are two of the park's best wildlife spots. Also good for quiet walks and photography.

Clematis South Lot

Quiet access 11300 block of Clematis Ln, north of Gasmer Drive

Closest to Willow Lake via the Kinder Foundation Trail. The quieter, less-visited of the two Clematis lots. Good for a peaceful shoreline walk with fewer people.

See Willow Lake → Get directions →

Gasmer Drive Lot

The prairie 4700 block of Gasmer Dr

Your gateway to Prairie Lake and the Native Prairie Preserve. Park here for the Cullen Foundation Trail loop and the boardwalk into the 15-acre preserve. Across South Post Oak Road from the other five lakes, a separate, quieter corner of the greenway.

Before You Arrive

Know before you go.

What to Bring

Must-haves:

  • Water (more than you think)
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Sun protection and a hat
  • Bug spray

Good to have:

  • Binoculars
  • Camera
  • Picnic supplies
  • Fishing gear (or borrow ours, email ahead)

Restrooms and Access

No restrooms on site. Plan ahead. The Dr. Walker Library nearby has indoor space and facilities.

Eight miles of ADA-accessible trails connect the lakes. The fishing pier at Westbury Lake is ADA-compliant. The Prairie Boardwalk is concrete and accessible.

While You're Here

A few things worth knowing.

Pack it in, pack it out

There are no trash services on the trails. Please carry out everything you bring and help keep the park beautiful.

Dogs on leash

Dogs are welcome and loved here. Please keep them leashed at all times and use the waste stations.

Wildlife etiquette

Watch wildlife from a distance and never feed it, except the turtles at Turtle Bridge: corn or peas only, never bread.

Trail courtesy

Trails are shared by walkers, runners, and cyclists. Ride at a friendly pace and give a heads-up when passing.

Know your poison ivy

"Leaves of three, let it be." It's a normal part of the ecosystem, so give it space and stay on established paths.

Turtle Bridge tip

Watch the turtles from the bridge. And please, no bread. Corn or peas only, if you feed them at all.

In the Area

Make a morning or a day of it.

  • Coffee and morning start: Tapester's Grill, Fioza Café
  • Quick meals: Annie's Hamburgers, The Hungry Farmer BBQ, Lo Nuevo De Jalisco
  • Evening: A Drop of the Creator (distillery, cocktails, pizza, outdoor seating)
  • Supplies: Kroger, Foodarama, Academy Sports + Outdoors
  • Cool down: Dr. Walker Library (indoor space, restrooms)
