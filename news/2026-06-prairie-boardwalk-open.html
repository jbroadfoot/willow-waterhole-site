The Prairie Boardwalk Is Open

For years, one of the rarest landscapes in Texas sat behind a fence at the edge of Willow Waterhole: visible, protected, and just out of reach. That changes now. The Prairie Boardwalk is open, and for the first time, you can walk a third of a mile into the heart of the Native Prairie Preserve without a single footstep touching the fragile ground itself.

The preserve protects 15 acres of native coastal prairie, an ecosystem that once covered more than nine million acres of the upper Texas coast and of which only about one percent remains. Surveys have identified 265 native plant species here, including the federally endangered Texas Prairie Dawn and two threatened species that grow almost nowhere else on Earth.

The concrete boardwalk, funded by a 2023 grant from the Texas Parks & Wildlife Department, lets visitors experience that rarity up close while keeping the mima mounds and sparse soils below undisturbed. Interpretive panels along the route tell the story of the prairie, its plants and pollinators, the birds of the Central Flyway, and the flood-control engineering that shares this ground. An outdoor classroom at the center of the preserve welcomes school groups and visitors of every age.

Plan your walk

The boardwalk is open dawn to dusk, every day, free of charge. Park at the Gasmer Drive lot (4700 block of Gasmer Dr), closest to Prairie Lake, and follow the signs to the Native Prairie Preserve. Spring brings wildflowers; early morning brings the birds; golden hour brings the photographers.
